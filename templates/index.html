FIFA Rankings and the chances of victory

FIFA, the world governing body for soccer, developed a ranking scheme for international teams that started in 1993. The method assigns points to teams after each match, and the number of points added or subtracted to related to the match result, the strength of the opponent, and the importance of the match.

The FIFA rankings have been heavily criticised, but the plot shows they can be useful predictors of soccer results. International match results from 1993 to present day were compared with the difference in FIFA ranking between two teams at the time the match was played. A probability of victory was calculated from this data, showing that higher ranked teams have a better chance of winning a match.

Teams at the very top and very bottom of the FIFA rankings rarely play each other, and the small sample size introduces noise into the probability analysis. Of course, the higher ranked teams are heavily favored in such cases.

Teams that qualify for the World Cup play a group stage of usually 3 games, and results at this stage determine if the team progresses or returns home. 3 points are awarded to teams for a victory, 1 point for a draw, and 0 for a loss. The data from the international matches show that there is at most about a 1 in 4 chance of a match being drawn, and the probability decreases the further teams are separated according to the FIFA rankings.
