Expanded financial resources in modern soccer

The FIFA World Cup takes place every 4 years, and the next edition will be in Russia in the summer of 2018. Only Westen European and South American nations have participated in the finals of all World Cups, but other nations have made significant investments in the sport. The investment in the last 3 decades has had an impact in professional soccer, but it is unclear how this translates to international competitions. Professional soccer clubs sign players to contracts of varied lengths. A club can only sign a player from another club if a transfer fee is agreed on and paid to the player's current team. The surge in financial resources in the last few decades has seen an increase in transfer fees paid, with the world record transfer fee surging within the last 15 years.

Does birth month matter?

There was an interesting study on the bias in the NHL for players born in the first 3 months of the year. The most intersting aspect was that players born at the beginning of the year were a little older than those in their age group during their school years, so they were selected for school teams due to being a little stronger, faster and bigger. These selections offered them the opportunity to practice more, to the point where more could play professionally. There were more World Cup soccer players were born in the first 3 months of the year, but this is unlikely to have any significant role in performance at the World Cup.
